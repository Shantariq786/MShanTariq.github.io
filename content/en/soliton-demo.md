intro: >-
  This interactive visualization shows a two-soliton interaction for the Korteweg-de Vries equation, \(u_t + 6u u_x + u_{xxx} = 0\). The taller soliton travels faster, overtakes the smaller one, and both re-emerge with their identities preserved after the collision.

highlights:
  - title: "Elastic Interaction"
    description: "The two solitary waves briefly overlap, but they do not lose their characteristic structure."
  - title: "Preserved Profiles"
    description: "After the interaction, each soliton recovers its localized shape rather than dispersing away."
  - title: "Phase Shift"
    description: "The collision changes the effective position of each soliton, even though the overall profiles remain intact."

note: >-
  This demo is intended as an intuitive visual explanation for readers. It highlights one of the defining features of soliton solutions: nonlinear interaction without permanent loss of shape.
